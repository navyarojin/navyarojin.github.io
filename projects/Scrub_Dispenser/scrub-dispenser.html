Non-Contact Scrub Dispensing System

IEEE EMBS Internship • BCMCH Thiruvalla

This project was developed during my 2nd-year summer internship at BCMCH Thiruvalla under IEEE EMBS. The goal was to design a low-cost, non-contact scrub dispensing system using IR sensing and a peristaltic pump. The system eliminates contamination and prevents clogging, while avoiding expensive electronics — making it suitable for high-usage clinical environments.

Key Features

  • IR-based proximity detection for touch-free dispensing
  • Peristaltic pump to avoid fluid backflow & clogging
  • Low-cost analog control (no microcontroller required)
  • Custom PCB with comparator-based control
  • 3D-printed enclosure with screw-on fluid bottle mount
  • Designed for medical scrub fluid usage in hospitals
